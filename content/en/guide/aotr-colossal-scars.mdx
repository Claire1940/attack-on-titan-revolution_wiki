The Colossal raid in AOT:R is mechanically different from standard raid loops. You enter with a defense objective and a boss race happening at the same time. If your team ignores either one, the run can collapse quickly.

| Core Mechanic | What It Means | Why It Matters for Farming |

## Phase-by-Phase Strategy (What to Do and When)

Clean execution is more important than flashy mechanics. Use this flow every run until your group can repeat it without voice comm confusion.

### Phase 1: Cannon Stall and Objective Control

1. Skip intro and deploy immediately.
2. Put your assigned damage buffer online early.
3. Cannon players aim slightly above at longer range, then lower as Colossal approaches.
4. Defender rotates to Eren-side wave lanes and clears fast threats first.
5. Avoid random overextension; the goal is controlled HP reduction.

### Phase 2: Transition at 50% HP

At roughly half health, the fight opens into heavier ODM pressure windows. This is where many runs fail because teams panic and stack into danger zones.

| Transition Check | Correct Action |
|---|---|
| **Colossal enters ability cycle** | Back off, preserve gas/blades, wait for vulnerability |
| **Eren side overwhelmed** | Send one burst player to peel temporarily |
| **Team ammo low** | Shift to blade discipline, avoid wasteful spear spam |
| **Cooldowns available** | Coordinate a short synchronized burst window |

### Phase 3: Burst Windows and Survival

The boss frequently uses long ability sequences and can feel “untouchable” during those moments. Your job is patience, not desperation.

- Wait out steam/immunity behavior.
- Re-engage only after visual confirmation of vulnerable state.
- Dump high-value cooldowns quickly.
- Disengage before the next ability chain.

> **Tip:** Think in cycles: **disengage → confirm window → burst → disengage**. This rhythm improves both survival and total damage over time.
