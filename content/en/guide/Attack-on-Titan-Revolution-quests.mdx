## Types of Attack on Titan Revolution Quests

The quest system in AOT Revolution is divided into several categories, each serving a specific purpose for player growth. Understanding the frequency and reward structure of these tasks allows you to prioritize your playtime effectively.

| Quest Category | Frequency | Primary Rewards | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

### Main and Side Quests
Main quests are designed to guide you through the initial mechanics of the game. These are generally focused on reaching specific level milestones or completing certain maps for the first time. Side quests often mirror these requirements but offer additional incentives to explore different gameplay features, such as customizing your character or joining a regiment. Because these can only be completed once per account, it is recommended to save the XP rewards for when you are pushing toward a specific level cap.

### Recurring Daily and Weekly Tasks
To maintain a steady flow of Gems for family spins, you must focus on the recurring **Attack on Titan Revolution quests**. Daily tasks often involve killing a specific number of Titans or completing a set amount of missions on any difficulty. Weekly quests are more demanding, potentially requiring you to tackle high-grade missions or achieve specific kill streaks. 

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your quest log immediately upon logging in. Some daily quests can be completed simultaneously while farming for artifacts or prestige scrolls.

## Maximizing Gem Income Through Quests

Gems are the lifeblood of your progression. They allow you to buy spins, which are necessary to roll for high-tier families like Ackerman or Reiss. The Battle Pass itself then unlocks further quests across eight weeks, creating a cycle of rewards that can significantly boost your luck and gold find stats.

## Mission Grades and Equipment Requirements

To participate in the more lucrative **Attack on Titan Revolution quests**, you must ensure your equipment meets the required grade. The game calculates your eligibility based on your average equipment level rather than a single high-stat item.

| Mission Difficulty | Required Grade | Recommended Skills |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Easy** | F to E- | Train Cadet, Hand Grinder |
| **Normal** | E+ | Hardcounter, Rising Slash |
| **Hard** | D to C | Lethal Tempo, Cooldown I |
| **Abnormal** | B+ | Defense Tree Focus, Cooldown II |

If you find yourself unable to select a mission for a quest, check your Equipment tab. You may need to upgrade your blades, gas canisters, or ODM gear to bring your average grade up to the necessary threshold. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** High-difficulty missions in 2026 feature Titans that can one-shot players. Investing in the defense side of the skill tree is no longer optional for late-game questing.

## Essential Skills for Quest Efficiency

Completing quests faster means more rewards per hour. To optimize your Titan-slaying efficiency, certain skills in the skill tree are considered essential.

1.  **Hand Grinder:** This is your primary escape tool. If a Titan grabs you during a mission, this skill allows you to break free and continue the quest.
2.  **Hardcounter:** This skill serves a dual purpose by allowing you to escape a grab while simultaneously killing the Titan.
3.  **Lethal Tempo:** Widely regarded as one of the best attack skills, Lethal Tempo allows you to chain kills together, which is vital for quests requiring high kill counts in a single run.
4.  **Rising Slash:** Excellent for dealing massive area-of-effect (AoE) damage to clusters of Titans, especially in defensive missions.

## Achievements and Artifact Missions

Beyond the standard quest log, the Achievement tab (located at the top right of the screen) offers long-term goals that function similarly to **Attack on Titan Revolution quests**. These achievements are categorized into different "feeds" and "masteries."

*   **Scout Domination:** Focuses on map completion and total Titan kills.
*   **Technique Mastery:** Requires precision and the use of specific skills to down Titans.
*   **Scout Feats:** These are the most difficult challenges in the game, rewarding players with powerful artifacts like the **Crown**.

Artifacts are unique items that provide stat boosts, such as skill cooldown reduction or increased damage. Unlike perks, which can be crafted or dropped, the best artifacts are almost exclusively tied to completing these high-level achievement quests.

## Prestige and the Quest for Luck

Once you reach the maximum level, the Prestige system becomes your primary focus. Each prestige level increases your level cap by 25 and allows you to choose a permanent buff. When completing your prestige-related **Attack on Titan Revolution quests**, it is highly recommended to choose **Luck**.

Luck influences almost every drop rate in the game, from the rarity of perks you find in missions to the chance of obtaining the elusive Attack Serum (the item required for Titan shifting). In the current 2026 meta, a high Luck stat is the difference between farming for weeks and finding your desired items in a few hours.

### The Market and Shop Cycle
While questing, keep an eye on the Market. The shop refreshes every six hours (four times daily). It is generally advised to avoid spending Gems on random perk cases. Instead, use your hard-earned Gems from quests to buy spins or save up for the Battle Pass, which offers a more guaranteed return on investment.

## FAQ

**Q: How often do Attack on Titan Revolution quests reset?**

A: Daily quests reset every 24 hours, while weekly quests reset every seven days. The Battle Pass quests are released in weekly increments over an eight-week period during a season.

**Q: Why can't I start a mission even though I have the right level?**

A: Missions are locked based on your average equipment grade, not just your player level. Ensure all your gear (Blades, ODM, Gas) is upgraded to meet the minimum grade requirement (e.g., E+ for Normal difficulty).

**Q: What is the fastest way to get Gems from quests?**

A: The fastest way is to stack your Daily and Weekly quests. Many of them have overlapping requirements, such as "Kill 50 Titans" and "Complete 5 Missions." By playing on maps with Luck or Gold boosts, you maximize the efficiency of every run.

**Q: Are side quests worth doing in 2026?**

A: Yes. While they are one-time rewards, the Gems provided by side quests are essential for early-game spins, helping you secure a decent family buff before you begin the more difficult prestige grind.
