## Character Creation and the Family System

Before you can even set foot on the battlefield, you must customize your character. The most critical aspect of this process is your Family. Families in this game are not just cosmetic; they provide unique skills, stat buffs, and passive abilities that define your playstyle. These are obtained through "spins" or "rolls," which can be purchased with gems or earned through gameplay.

Families are categorized by rarity: Common, Rare, Epic, and Legendary. While a Legendary family like the Ackermans or Reiss provides a massive advantage, new players should focus on building a solid foundation regardless of their initial roll.

| Family Rarity | Acquisition Method | Typical Buffs |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Common** | High drop rate from spins | Minor stamina or health boosts |
| **Rare** | Moderate drop rate | Increased ODM speed or gas efficiency |
| **Epic** | Low drop rate | Significant damage multipliers and unique passives |
| **Legendary** | Extremely low (often &lt;0.5%) | Game-changing skills and massive stat scaling |

> **💡 Tip:** Do not waste all your early-game gems on family spins unless you have a specific build in mind. It is often better to invest in the Battle Pass or equipment upgrades first.

## Resource Management: Gems and Spins

To progress in 2026, you will need a steady supply of Gems and Spins. Gems are the primary currency used for purchasing the Battle Pass, equipment, and cosmetic cases. Spins are used exclusively for rerolling your family and perks.

There are several ways to accumulate these resources:
1.  **Quests:** Complete Daily, Weekly, and Side quests for consistent rewards.
2.  **Achievements:** Check the top right of your screen for the achievement tab. Completing "Scout Domination" or "Technique Mastery" provides massive one-time gem payouts.
3.  **The AFK Zone:** For players who want to earn while they sleep, the AFK zone allows you to accumulate gems over time. Some advanced players use multi-instance setups to maximize this gain, though this is taxing on hardware.
4.  **Codes:** Join the official Discord channel to stay updated on new codes released by the developers, which often grant hundreds of free spins.

## Optimization and Performance Settings

Because this game relies heavily on timing and frame-perfect ODM movement, performance is key. If you experience lag, your ability to dodge titan grabs will be severely compromised.

*   **Graphics:** Turn off blur, player effects, and shadows to maintain a high frame rate.
*   **Gameplay:** Disable all invites except for friends.
*   **Mobile Players:** Ensure "NA Assist" is enabled to help with targeting on touchscreens.

## Equipment and Grades: Your Attack on Titan Revolution Tutorial for Scaling

One of the most common mistakes new players make is focusing on a single stat rather than their overall Equipment Grade. To unlock higher difficulty missions, your *average* equipment level must meet the requirement. For example, a Normal difficulty mission might require an **E+ Grade**. Even if your blades are top-tier, if your gas canisters are low-level, your average grade will prevent you from entering.

| Equipment Part | Primary Function | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blades** | Determines damage dealt to the nape | **High** |
| **ODM Gear** | Controls movement speed and reel strength | **Medium** |
| **Gas Canisters** | Determines how long you can stay airborne | **Medium** |
| **Cloak/Armor** | Provides defense and survivability | **Low (Early Game)** |

## Essential Skills for Survival

The skill tree is divided into Offensive, Defensive, and Utility branches. While it is tempting to go full damage, being "one-shot" is a real risk in high-level missions. Following this **Attack on Titan Revolution tutorial** strategy, you should prioritize a balanced build that allows for both escape and execution.

### Recommended Early-Game Skills
*   **Hand Grinder:** This is your primary escape tool. If a titan grabs you, this skill allows you to break free and avoid an instant death.
*   **Hardcounter:** A powerful reactive skill that allows you to kill a titan at the same time you escape its grasp.
*   **Rising Slash:** Excellent for dealing area-of-effect damage to multiple titans clustered together.
*   **Lethal Tempo:** Arguably the best offensive skill in the game. It allows you to chain kills together, potentially taking down three titans with minimal effort.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not ignore the defense side of the skill tree. Even at max level, titans in higher difficulties can end your run in a single hit if you haven't invested in survivability.

## The Prestige System and End-Game Progression

Once you reach the level cap, you have the option to Prestige. Each prestige level increases your level cap by 25, allowing for further stat growth. However, prestiging resets your progress, so you must prepare beforehand.

Before you hit the prestige button, ensure you have a stack of **Prestige Scrolls**. These can be purchased in the Market or farmed in the Everend map. When you prestige, you are given a choice between Luck, Gold, and XP boosts. 

In the current 2026 meta, **always choose Luck**. Luck scales with every drop in the game, including the rare items needed for Titan Shifting. After selecting Luck, you will choose "Memories," which are permanent buffs that persist through your next prestige cycle.

## Advanced Mechanics: Artifacts and Shifting

For those looking to reach the pinnacle of power, Artifacts and Titan Shifting are the ultimate goals. Artifacts are rare items that provide massive stat boosts, such as skill cooldown reduction or raw damage.

*   **The Crown:** Widely considered the best artifact in the game, obtained through the "Scout Feats" achievement.
*   **Titan Shifting:** To transform into a titan, you need the **Attack Serum**. This item has an incredibly low drop rate (often around 0.24% per mission). To increase your chances, focus on high-difficulty raids and ensure your Luck stat is as high as possible.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more spins for my family in Attack on Titan Revolution?**

A: You can obtain spins by using codes provided by the developers on Discord, completing daily and weekly quests, or purchasing them with gems in the market. As mentioned in this **Attack on Titan Revolution tutorial**, joining the AFK zone is also a viable way to farm the gems needed for spins.

**Q: What is the best stat to focus on when prestiging?**

A: Luck is universally considered the best stat to pick. It affects the drop rates of perks, artifacts, and the elusive Attack Serum, making it essential for long-term progression.

**Q: Why can't I join a Hard difficulty mission even though my blades are high level?**

A: Missions check your **Average Equipment Grade**, not just your blades. You must upgrade your ODM gear, gas canisters, and other equipment to ensure your total average meets the mission requirement.

**Q: Are regiments worth the 150 Robux?**

A: Joining a regiment is primarily for social play and competitive rankings. If you want to earn the "Champion" achievement or participate in organized raids, joining a regiment is a great investment, but it is not strictly necessary for solo progression.
