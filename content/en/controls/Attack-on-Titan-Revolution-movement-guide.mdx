## The Fundamentals: Single Hook vs. Double Hook

One of the most common mistakes beginners make is "double hooking"—firing both grappling hooks simultaneously. While this might feel like it provides more stability, it is actually a significant tactical disadvantage in high-stakes combat.

Every hook in the game has an internal cooldown. When you use both hooks at once, they both go on cooldown at the same time. If a Crawler or an Aberrant lunges at you during this window, you are left completely defenseless and unable to maneuver away. Conversely, utilizing a single hook allows you to alternate. If you miss your target or need to readjust, you always have a second hook ready to go.

| Hooking Method | Cooldown Risk | Adjustability | Recommended Skill Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Single Hook** | Low (Alternating) | **Maximum** | Beginner to Pro |
| **Double Hook** | High (Simultaneous) | **Minimum** | Not Recommended |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid double hooking when fighting Crawlers. Their erratic movement requires constant hook readjustment, which is impossible if both your lines are on cooldown.

## Staying Airborne: Aerial Control and Height Management

Staying in the air is not just about firing hooks; it is about managing your verticality. To maintain a high vantage point, you should look toward the sky and perform a double boost. Once you have reached your desired altitude, you can use specific key combinations to survey the battlefield.

### The "S-Key" Adjustment
Pressing your **S key two times** while in the air will push your character backward and slightly higher. This is an excellent way to reset your perspective. After killing a Titan, many players use this double-tap to jump back, giving them a clear view of the next target's location and nape orientation.

### Using the W-Key for Gas
If your hooks cannot reach a distant building or Titan, you can hold the **W key** to use your gas. This propels you forward and upward, helping you clear obstacles or reach high-altitude grapple points.

| Key Bind | Action | Tactical Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Double Space** | Air Boost | Initial lift and speed bursts |
| **S + S** | Backwards Jump | Resetting aim and surveying the map |
| **W (Hold)** | Gas Propulsion | Gaining height and clearing buildings |
| **A / D** | Directional Swing | Orbiting Titans for nape access |

## Advanced Speed: Spam Boosting and Mega Boosting

To move across the map quickly, you need to master the art of the "Spam Boost." This involves timing your spacebar presses to maintain momentum without triggering a "Mega Boost," which can sometimes be harder to control in tight spaces.

1. **Start the Momentum**: Double space in the air to get your initial lift.
2. **Hook and Release**: Attach a hook to a distant flat plane or building.
3. **The Rhythm**: Rapidly tap the spacebar and occasionally the W key. This allows you to stay high in the air while moving at maximum velocity.
4. **Mega Boost Timing**: If you press space fast enough during specific animations, you will perform a Mega Boost. This is useful for long-distance travel but requires precise timing to avoid crashing into geometry.

## Combat Movement: Three Levels of Titan Slaying

Movement isn't just for travel; it is your primary weapon. This Attack on Titan Revolution movement guide categorizes Titan kills into three distinct difficulty levels based on the complexity of the maneuvers required.

### 1. Beginner Level: The Eye-to-Nape Transition
This is the safest method for new players. You first hook the Titan's eye to blind it, which temporarily freezes its movement. Once the Titan is stunned, you swing around to the back and hook the nape. While slow, it ensures you aren't grabbed out of the air.

### 2. Intermediate Level: The Directional Swing
Experienced players use the **A or D keys** to orbit the Titan. By hooking right below the chin and holding a directional key, you can pull yourself in a curve. As you pass the nape, you release the key and strike. This is particularly effective against Aberrants, whose erratic hand movements make direct approaches risky.

### 3. Professional Level: The Close-Quarters Descent
This is the preferred method for speedrunners aiming for sub-2:30 mission times. Instead of swinging wide, you hook the side of the neck directly. You stay as close to the hitbox as possible, descending only at the final millisecond to strike. 

| Difficulty | Technique | Best Used For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Easy** | Blinding Eyes | New players and safe play |
| **Medium** | Directional Orbiting | Aberrants and general combat |
| **Hard** | Neck-Close Strike | Speedrunning and high-level farming |

> **💡 Tip:** The hitbox for a Titan's nape is actually larger than the visual model. You don't always need to be perfectly behind them; striking the side of the neck often registers as a kill.

## Optimization and Hitbox Mastery

To truly excel, you must understand how the game registers your position. The closer you are to the neck, the faster your travel time to the kill zone. When executing high-speed kills, aim your hook at the side of the neck rather than the base of the chin. This reduces the distance your character needs to travel to reach the "critical zone."

If you find yourself overextending (flying past the Titan), use the opposite directional key to pull yourself back in. For example, if you are swinging right and realize you are going too wide, tap the **D key** to tighten your arc toward the nape.

## FAQ

**Q: Why do I keep running out of gas so quickly?**

A: You are likely holding the W key or spacebar for too long. Effective movement in Attack on Titan Revolution relies on short, rhythmic bursts of gas rather than constant expenditure. Try to use your hooks for momentum and your gas only for height adjustments.

**Q: What is the best way to avoid being grabbed by a Titan?**

A: Always maintain a high altitude. Use the "S-S" jump back technique after every kill to get above the reach of nearby Titans. If you stay at the level of their shoulders, you are in the "danger zone" for grabs and swipes.

**Q: How do I perform a Mega Boost consistently?**

A: Mega Boosting is all about the timing of your spacebar taps relative to your hook release. It requires a very high "Actions Per Minute" (APM). Practice hooking a flat surface and tapping space as fast as possible to find the window where your character receives a massive forward velocity increase.

**Q: Does the Attack on Titan Revolution movement guide work for controllers?**

A: While this guide uses keyboard terminology (WASD), the principles are the same. Use the left analog stick for directional orbiting and the jump button for boosting. Single-hooking remains the superior strategy regardless of your input device.
