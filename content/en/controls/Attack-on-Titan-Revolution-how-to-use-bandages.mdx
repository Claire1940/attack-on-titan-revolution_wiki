## Mastering Attack on Titan Revolution: How to Use Bandages and Heal

The primary function of bandages in Attack on Titan Revolution is to counteract the "Bleeding" status effect. In this game, taking damage—especially from a Titan grab or a direct swat—often results in a lingering bleed that drains your health over time. Unlike some RPGs where health regenerates instantly, this game requires manual intervention to stop the bleeding before you can focus on regaining your HP.

### Step-by-Step: Using Bandages in Combat

To use a bandage effectively, follow these steps during a mission:

1.  **Identify the Bleed:** Look for red visual cues on your screen or a specific icon in your status bar indicating you are losing health over time.
2.  **Find a Safe Opening:** You cannot use bandages while actively swinging on your ODM gear or in the middle of an attack animation. Use your momentum to land on a rooftop or a tall tree branch.
3.  **Select the Item:** Depending on your keybinds (usually '3' or '4' on PC), select the bandage from your quick-access inventory.
4.  **Hold the Use Key:** Press and hold the interaction key (typically 'E' or the designated item button) to begin the healing animation.
5.  **Monitor the Bar:** A small progress bar will appear. Once it fills, the bleeding stops, and a portion of your health is restored.

| Action | Control (PC) | Control (Console) | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Select Bandage** | Number Keys (3-6) | D-Pad Cycle | Equips item to hand |
| **Apply Bandage** | Hold 'E' or Left Click | Hold 'X' or R2 | Stops Bleeding / Restores HP |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Being hit while the bandage animation is active will cancel the heal and consume the item. Always ensure you are at a safe distance from active Titans before attempting to patch up.

## Enhancing Survival: The Defense Skill Tree

While knowing **Attack on Titan Revolution how to use bandages** is vital, the best strategy is to reduce the frequency with which you need them. The game features a robust skill tree divided into Offense and Defense. For 2026 gameplay, neglecting the defense side is a common mistake that leads to "one-shot" deaths in high-level missions.

### Key Skills for Longevity
To maximize the effectiveness of your bandages, you should invest in skills that allow you to escape damage entirely or mitigate the severity of hits.

*   **Hand Grinder:** This is a mandatory skill for escaping when a Titan grabs you. Escaping quickly reduces the duration of the "Bleeding" effect, meaning you'll need fewer bandages to recover.
*   **Hard Counter:** This allows you to escape a grab and kill the Titan simultaneously. It is the ultimate defensive-offensive hybrid.
*   **Defense Tier 1 & 2:** These passive nodes increase your overall health pool and damage resistance. With higher resistance, a single bandage can restore a larger percentage of your effective health.

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize "Hand Grinder" in your early skill points. It is the single most important tool for preventing fatal damage that bandages cannot fix in time.

## Equipment Grades and Healing Efficiency

Your equipment grade (E+, D, C, etc.) directly impacts your survivability. As you progress to higher difficulty missions, the damage dealt by Titans scales significantly. If your average equipment level is low, you will find yourself using bandages constantly.

In 2026, the game calculates your mission eligibility based on your **average equipment grade**, not just your highest-level piece. If your ODM damage is high but your durability is low, you will be a "glass cannon" that requires a full stack of bandages just to survive a single encounter.

| Equipment Component | Impact on Survival | Bandage Synergy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blades** | Faster kills = less damage taken | Reduces need for healing |
| **ODM Gear** | Better mobility / dodging | Helps find safe spots to heal |
| **Reinforced Suits** | Higher DMG resistance | Makes each bandage more valuable |

## Advanced Survival: Perks and Prestige

Beyond basic skills, your "Perks" and "Prestige" choices influence how often you'll be reaching for your medical kit. Perks range from Common to Legendary and can be crafted or found in high-grade missions.

### Choosing the Right Perks
If you find yourself struggling with health, look for perks that boost **Defense** or **HP Regen**. In the 2026 update, some legendary perks even offer a "Lifesteal" mechanic or a chance to automatically stop bleeding without using a bandage.

### Prestige and Luck
When you reach the level cap and decide to Prestige, you are given a choice between Luck, Gold, and XP. **Always choose Luck.** Higher luck not only gives you better family rolls and artifact drops but also increases the drop rate of high-tier bandages and medical supplies during missions.

## Strategic Healing: When to Use Bandages

Knowing **Attack on Titan Revolution how to use bandages** also involves timing. In a chaotic battle with multiple 15-meter Titans, stopping to heal can be a death sentence.

1.  **The 50% Rule:** Generally, do not use a bandage if you are above 50% health unless you are currently bleeding. The animation lock is too risky for minor health gains.
2.  **The Bleed Priority:** If the bleed icon is active, you must find a way to heal immediately. Bleeding in Attack on Titan Revolution can quickly escalate, leaving you with 1 HP in a matter of seconds.
3.  **Teammate Cover:** If playing in a squad, call out when you are stopping to heal. A teammate can distract nearby Titans, giving you the 3-4 seconds required to complete the animation.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Rising Slash" skill to clear space around you before landing to use a bandage. This ensures no small Titans are lurking nearby to interrupt your healing.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I buy bandages in Attack on Titan Revolution?**

A: You can purchase bandages at the Market using Gold. The shop inventory rotates every 6 hours, so keep an eye out for bulk deals. You can also find them in supply crates during missions.

**Q: Can I use bandages while moving on ODM gear?**

A: No. Using a bandage requires a stationary animation. You must land on a flat surface or a branch to successfully apply the item. Attempting to use it while swinging will fail.

**Q: How do I stop the bleeding effect without a bandage?**

A: In the current 2026 version of the game, bleeding will eventually stop on its own after a long duration, but it usually results in massive health loss. Certain high-tier Perks or Family skills (like those from the Ackerman or Reiss families) may provide faster recovery or bleed resistance.

**Q: Does the "Hand Grinder" skill use a bandage?**

A: No, Hand Grinder is a skill that uses your blades to escape a Titan's grip. However, escaping a grip usually leaves you bleeding, so you will likely need to know **Attack on Titan Revolution how to use bandages** immediately after performing a Hand Grinder escape.
