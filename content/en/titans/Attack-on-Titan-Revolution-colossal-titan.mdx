The path to becoming a Colossal Titan shifter is the most difficult challenge currently available in the game. Unlike other serums that might be found through standard missions, the Colossal Serum is tied specifically to the Colossal Raid. This raid is not for the faint of heart; it is locked behind the highest difficulty tier, requiring players to be in a Baron A-grade session to even attempt the drop.

To successfully farm the serum, you should prepare for a low drop rate and a high-intensity battle. The raid is designed around team coordination rather than solo brute force. While some elite players have attempted to solo the encounter, the dual-objective nature of the mission makes it nearly impossible without a dedicated squad.

## The Colossal Raid Strategy

The Colossal Raid is a multi-phase encounter that tests both your ODM skills and your tactical positioning. You can find the raid entrance in the main menu under the "Raids" tab, but remember it must be set to the highest difficulty to yield the best rewards.

### Phase 1: The Wall Defense
In the first half of the raid, the Colossal Titan is walking toward the wall. You cannot damage his nape during this phase; your blades will deal zero damage. Instead, players must utilize the cannons placed along the wall. Aim for the chest and stomach area to maximize damage. While the "cannon crew" focuses on the boss, at least one player must stay on the ground to protect Eren from waves of smaller Titans. If Eren dies or the Colossal Titan touches the wall, the mission is a failure.

### Phase 2: The Direct Confrontation
Once the Colossal Titan reaches 50% health, the fight transitions into a standard battle. However, "standard" is a loose term here. The boss will utilize high-pressure steam to knock players back and deal massive HP drain. Shifting into your own Titan form during this phase is highly recommended to tank hits and provide the necessary DPS to finish him off.

## Mastery Cards and Quality of Life

One of the best additions in the recent update is the "Upgrade All" button and the introduction of Titan Mastery Cards. Previously, players had to spend hours manually clicking through skill nodes and farming thousands of kills to max out a Titan. 

Now, if you obtain a Titan Card (which can drop from missions or raids), you can instantly boost a skill's level. For example, a single card can jump a skill from level 3 to level 13 instantly. This drastically reduces the entry barrier for players who just unlocked the **Attack on Titan Revolution colossal titan** and want to be competitive immediately.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Mastery Cards are rare. Save them for the higher-level nodes of the Colossal Titan tree, as those require the most experience to unlock naturally.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get the Colossal Serum in Attack on Titan Revolution?**

A: The Colossal Serum is a rare drop from the Colossal Raid. You must play on the highest difficulty (Baron A-grade or higher) for a chance to obtain it. It is not available in easier difficulty tiers.

**Q: Can I solo the Colossal Raid?**

A: While technically possible for the top 0.1% of players, it is extremely difficult because you must defend Eren from small Titans while simultaneously using cannons to stop the Colossal Titan. A team of 3-4 players is highly recommended.

**Q: What is the best family for the Attack on Titan Revolution colossal titan?**

A: The Shiki and Fritz families are currently the best choices. Shiki offers massive raw stat buffs, while Fritz provides excellent utility for Titan shifters. The 2026 update buffed both families significantly.

**Q: Do footsteps really deal damage as the Colossal Titan?**

A: Yes! One of the unique passives of the Colossal Titan is that simply walking near or over smaller Titans deals consistent damage. Additionally, both your transformation (nuke) and your de-transformation deal massive AOE damage to the surrounding area.
