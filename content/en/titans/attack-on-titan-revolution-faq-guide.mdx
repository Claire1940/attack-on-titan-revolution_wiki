## Best Early Progression Route (Levels, Quests, and Economy)

Your first objective is simple: build a stable loop for gems, XP, and unlock materials without random spending.

### Step-by-step early route

1. Finish mandatory customization and pick a playable family.
2. Clear one-time **main** and **side quests** for startup currency.
3. Rotate **daily** and **weekly** quests for steady gains.
4. Only enter mission tiers your average grade supports.
5. Save enough gems/spins for meaningful family upgrades.
6. Begin perk collection from higher-grade clears when your survival improves.

| Activity | Repeat Type | Main Reward | Efficiency for New Players |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Main Quests** | One-time | Gems + progression | **Excellent early** |
| **Side Quests** | One-time | Gems + utility progress | **Excellent early** |
| **Daily Quests** | Daily reset | Reliable currency | **High** |
| **Weekly Quests** | Weekly reset | Bigger burst rewards | **High** |

### Spending priorities for gems and spins

Use a “value checkpoint” approach:
- If your family is weak, allocate spins in controlled batches.
- If your family is already strong enough for your tier, prioritize progression systems (battle pass, upgrades, targeted gear growth).
- Avoid panic-spending after a bad roll streak.

## Skills, Perks, and Survivability Build Priorities

Damage gets attention, but survivability keeps your mission uptime high. If you fail less, you farm more.

Prioritize skills that:

Crafting can work, but it is expensive. Farming stronger missions for better drop quality is typically more resource-efficient once your clear rate is stable.

## Mission Scaling, Prestige, Artifacts, and Titan Shifting

This is where many players stall. They level up, but account power doesn’t keep pace.

### Mission modifiers and map choices

Pick maps with boosts that match your current goal:
- **Luck boost** when hunting rare drops
- **XP boost** when pushing level milestones
- **Gold boost** when preparing for crafting/upgrades

You can increase mission difficulty via modifiers for better rewards, but only do that after your survival rate is consistent.

| Goal | Best Map Bonus | Should You Use Harder Modifiers? |
|---|---|---|
| **Rare item farming** | **Luck** | Yes, if clear rate stays high |
| **Level pushing** | **XP** | Moderate difficulty first |
| **Currency farming** | **Gold** | Yes, if time-to-clear stays efficient |

### Prestige timing (critical for long-term growth)

Prestige unlocks after reaching cap and increases your future level ceiling. But timing matters:
- Stock required prestige-related materials first.
- Choose scaling options that improve long-term account value.
- Luck-focused paths are often favored because they influence multiple systems tied to drops and progression.

### Artifacts and achievement-based progression

Artifacts are not just side content. They can dramatically sharpen your build identity:
- cooldown-focused artifact stats for skill-spam setups,
- mixed survivability stats for consistency,
- targeted damage stats for confident mechanical players.

Some strong artifacts are tied to achievement chains, so don’t ignore challenge tracking tabs.

### Titan shifting and Attack Serum

Titan shifting progression depends on a rare serum-type drop. Expect low base drop rates unless you stack luck systems and optimize your farming environment. Treat this as a marathon objective, not a same-day unlock.

## Advanced Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced players fall into these traps:

- **Over-indexing damage:** If you die during approach windows, your DPS stat does nothing.
- **Ignoring average gear grade:** Mission access depends on total readiness, not one stat spike.
- **Prestiging without prep:** You lose momentum when you prestige with no material buffer.
- **Unplanned market spending:** Rotation urgency causes poor purchases.
- **No playstyle identity:** Random upgrades create weak hybrid builds.

A polished **attack on titan revolution faq guide** approach means planning around outcome, not impulse. Decide whether your account is in a farming phase, leveling phase, or rare-drop phase, then commit your stats and currency accordingly.

## FAQ

A: Start with average equipment grade targets, then build survivability skills, then scale perks. Use one goal per session (XP, luck, or gold) and choose map bonuses to match that goal.
