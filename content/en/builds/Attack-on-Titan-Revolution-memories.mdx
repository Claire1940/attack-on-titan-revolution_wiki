## Understanding Memory Categories and Rarity

Before diving into advanced acquisition strategies, you must understand how memories are classified. In Attack on Titan Revolution, memories are divided into three primary categories: Offense, Defense, and Support. Each category is represented by a specific icon color in your inventory and the skill tree.

Identifying the category of your desired memory is the first step toward targeting it. For example, if you are looking for "Tactician," you are looking for a Support memory. If you are hunting for "Gambler," you are in the Offense category.

| Memory Category | Icon Color | Primary Focus | Example Memories |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Offense** | Red | Damage, Crit, Speed | Gambler, Blood Thief |
| **Defense** | Blue | Health, Damage Reduction | Pantophobia, Life Feed |
| **Support** | Green | Cooldowns, Utility, Shifter | Tactician, Flash, Salt Feed |

The rarity of a memory is denoted by its star rating. While one-star and two-star memories are common, three-star and four-star memories offer significantly higher stat boosts but are much harder to obtain.

Once you have successfully pulled your desired **Attack on Titan Revolution memories**, the next step is synergy. Simply having a high-rarity memory isn't enough; it must complement your family buffs and skill tree choices.

### Recommended Memory Sets for 2026

Depending on your role in a squad, you should aim for specific combinations. In the current meta, hybrid builds are falling out of favor compared to "Hyper-Carry" or "Pure Support" roles.

*   **The Titan Killer (Offense):** Focus on memories like **Gambler** or **Blood Thief**. These reward aggressive playstyles and high kill counts with stacking damage buffs.
*   **The Immortal (Defense):** **Life Feed** combined with high health stats makes you nearly unkillable in high-tier raids.

## Advanced Memory Management

Managing your inventory is just as important as rolling. It is easy to accidentally delete a low-rarity memory that actually has a perfect stat roll for a niche build.

*   **Lock Your Favorites:** Always use the lock feature on three-star and four-star memories immediately after pulling them.
*   **Check Stats, Not Just Stars:** A well-rolled two-star memory can sometimes outperform a poorly-rolled three-star memory depending on the specific stat percentages.
*   **Pity System:** Keep track of your rolls. The game features a "Pity" mechanic where after a certain number of scrolls, you are guaranteed a higher rarity drop. Use the targeted skill tree trick right before you hit your pity count to ensure that guaranteed high-rarity drop is in the category you want.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use this trick for four-star Attack on Titan Revolution memories?**

A: Yes. The targeted skill tree method works for all rarities. While the 1% drop rate for four-star memories remains the same, this method ensures that when that 1% chance hits, it is much more likely to be the specific four-star memory you need for your category.

**Q: Does resetting my skill tree remove my current memories?**

A: No, resetting your skill tree only refunds your skill points and gold. Your currently equipped or stored memories will remain in your inventory. You simply won't benefit from the "Perk Slot" buffs until you reinvest your points.

**Q: What is the best memory for a Titan Shifter build in 2026?**

A: For most Shifter builds, **Tactician** (Support) and **Salt Feed** (Support) are highly recommended. These help with health management and cooldown reduction, allowing you to stay in Titan form longer and use your abilities more frequently.

**Q: Is it better to trade for scrolls or buy them with gems?**

A: In the current 2026 economy, trading high-value duplicate memories or family crates for scrolls is generally more efficient than spending gems directly, as gems are better saved for skill tree resets and laboratory upgrades.
