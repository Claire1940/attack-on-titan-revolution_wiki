## S-Tier Mythical Families: Fritz and Halas

For those who haven't yet unlocked Shikai, the Mythical families **Fritz** and **Halas** represent the pinnacle of the gacha system. These families are incredibly rare but offer game-changing passives.

### Fritz: The Founder's Power
Fritz is often cited as the **Attack on Titan Revolution best family** for players who want to focus entirely on Shifting. The "Founder's Vengeance" ability allows you to rebuild your body upon taking fatal damage, effectively granting you a second chance to shift and turn the tide of a battle.
*   **Key Buff:** 20% increase to all Titan stats.
*   **Active Ability:** Command pure Titans to ignore you or attack specific targets.

### Halas: The Unwavering Will
Halas is the ultimate hybrid family. It offers a massive 125 HP regeneration skill that also heals up to three injuries instantly. In a raid environment, Halas players can inspire their allies, granting a 15% boost to all ODM stats for 30 seconds. This makes them indispensable for high-level team play in 2026.

## A-Tier: Legendary Powerhouses

The Legendary tier is where most competitive players aim to land. These families offer specialized playstyles that can rival Mythicals if played correctly.

### Ackerman: The Human Weapon
The **Ackerman** family is unique because it removes the ability to shift into a Titan entirely. In exchange, you become a whirlwind of destruction. 
*   **Passive:** Extra offensive perk slot and a 20% damage boost in raids.
*   **Skills:** "Swift Strikes" allows you to eliminate up to five Titans in a single sequence, while "Lightning Bolt" enhances your Thunder Spears to pierce through multiple targets.

### Yeager: The Attack Titan
The **Yeager** family is the offensive counterpart to Fritz. It provides a 20% boost specifically to Attack Titan stats and a 15% critical chance increase. The "Invigorate" ability provides a second life, while "Berserk" mode (unlocked in the skill tree) increases your damage and speed as your heat rises.

## B-Tier and Support Families

While not as flashy as the top tiers, B-tier families like **Reiss** and **Braun** have specific niches. Reiss is particularly interesting as a support-focused lineage. It allows you to immobilize all pure Titans in a large radius using the "Conqueror" ability. For players who enjoy helping their team survive difficult waves, Reiss is a solid choice.

**Braun** and **Ksaver** offer defensive and Beast Titan-specific buffs, respectively. While they are reliable, they often lack the raw "carry" potential found in the Ackerman or Yeager lines. If you roll these early, they are worth keeping until you can afford a massive reroll session for an S-tier bloodline.

## How to Choose the Attack on Titan Revolution Best Family

Choosing the right family depends on your preferred role within a squad. In 2026, the game's difficulty has scaled such that a balanced team is often better than a group of pure damage dealers.

1.  **For Solo Players:** Aim for **Yeager** or **Ackerman**. The self-sufficiency of Yeager's "Invigorate" or Ackerman's raw damage output allows you to clear content without relying on a dedicated healer.
2.  **For Raid Leaders:** **Halas** or **Reiss** are the best choices. The ability to buff your entire team's ODM stats or freeze Titans in place can prevent a total party wipe in the hardest difficulties.
3.  **For Completionists:** Your goal is **Shikai**. Focus on clearing every achievement in the game to unlock this family, as it essentially combines the best traits of all other tiers.

## Why You Should Avoid Rare Families

It may be tempting to stick with a Rare family like **Finger** or **Tybur** if you like the character from the anime, but the math simply doesn't support it in 2026. Rare families offer minimal stat increases (usually around 5-10%) and often lack the "Shifter Regen" or unique active skills that define the higher tiers. If you find yourself stuck with a Rare family, focus on earning gold and gems to buy more spins as quickly as possible.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the Attack on Titan Revolution best family for beginners?**

A: For beginners, the **Arlert** or **Braun** families are excellent. They provide straightforward survival buffs and strong Titan stats that help you learn the game's mechanics without being too fragile. However, you should eventually aim for a Legendary or Mythical reroll.

**Q: Can I change my family after I have started the game?**

A: Yes, you can reroll your family at any time in the customization menu using Spins. Spins can be earned by completing quests, reaching milestones, or purchasing them with in-game currency.

**Q: Is the Ackerman family better than the Yeager family?**

A: It depends on your playstyle. **Ackerman** is better for players who prefer human combat and high-speed ODM maneuvers. **Yeager** is superior for those who want to utilize the Attack Titan's power and have a "safety net" with the second-life passive.

**Q: How do I unlock the Shikai family?**

A: Shikai is unlocked by completing the "Wings of Valor" achievement set. This involves finishing almost all other achievements in the game, making it the hardest but most rewarding family to obtain in 2026.
