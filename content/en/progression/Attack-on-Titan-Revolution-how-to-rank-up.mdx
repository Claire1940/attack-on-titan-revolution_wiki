## Attack on Titan Revolution how to rank up: Core Grade System Explained

In practical terms, your weapon’s overall grade behaves like a “lowest-link” system. Even if several stats are high, one neglected category can drag your displayed grade lower than expected.

Think of it this way: if six to eight upgrade categories exist on your equipment screen, your total grade is heavily influenced by the weakest category. So if most stats are around C but two are still E, your total may stay near the lower bracket.

| Grade Behavior | What It Means | What You Should Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Balanced stats** | Overall grade rises steadily | **Upgrade all categories in rounds** |
| **One-stat focus** | Grade gains slow or stall | **Stop maxing a single line early** |
| **Low outlier stat** | Outlier limits total grade | **Bring weakest category up first** |
| **Even mid-tier spread** | Better value per cash spent | **Push all lines to same tier before next jump** |

> **Warning:** If you only upgrade blade damage while ignoring durability/mobility lines, you can spend a lot of cash and still feel “stuck” on grade progression.

## Step-by-Step Upgrade Route (Early to Late Game)

Follow these steps to get consistent grade increases without burning all your currency.

### 1) Open Equipment and check every upgrade line
Start from your active weapon and scan each upgrade row. Note which lines are lowest (for example, E- while others are E or D-).

### 2) Upgrade in “tiers,” not in single-stat streaks
Bring all lines to the same tier first:
- E- → E across all lines  
- E → E+ across all lines  
- E+ → D- across all lines

### 3) Use a weakest-first rotation
Each time you earn enough cash for 2–4 upgrades, spend on the lowest categories first. This keeps overall grade moving.

### 4) Set a cash reserve
Don’t spend down to zero after each run. Keep enough for at least one “catch-up” upgrade on a lagging stat.

### 5) Re-check grade breakpoint after each round
After completing a full round (all lines improved once), verify if your gear grade tier changed.

## Best Cash Efficiency Strategy for Ranking Up Faster

Most rank-up problems are really cash-allocation problems. You can play a lot but progress slowly if you buy upgrades in the wrong order.

### Prioritize “grade impact per cost”
When deciding between upgrades, ask:
1. Is this currently one of my lowest categories?
2. Is this upgrade cost lower than the rest?
3. Will this complete a tier across multiple lines?

If yes to at least two, buy it first.

### Practical spending framework

| Decision Type | Good Choice | Bad Choice | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **First purchase after run** | Cheapest low-tier stat | Most expensive high-tier stat | Raises weakest link quickly |
| **When one stat lags** | Catch it up immediately | Ignore it for damage upgrades | Prevents grade bottleneck |
| **When costs spike** | Save for round completion | Random upgrades as soon as affordable | Keeps grade growth predictable |
| **Before logging off** | End on balanced spread | End with big stat gaps | Better next-session momentum |

A simple routine:
- Run missions for cash
- Return to equipment
- Upgrade the 2–3 lowest lines
- Repeat

That rhythm is more effective than chasing one big “power spike” purchase.

## Common Mistakes That Slow Rank Progression

Players often think they need rare luck or secret mechanics for **Attack on Titan Revolution how to rank up**, but most issues come from avoidable habits.

### Mistake 1: Overcommitting to one stat
High damage feels good immediately, but grade progression may lag behind if other lines stay low.

### Mistake 2: Upgrading without a checkpoint plan
If you don’t define your current target (e.g., “all lines to D”), you’ll spend inefficiently.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring small upgrades
Lower-cost upgrades often provide the best short-term grade movement because they fix weak links.

### Mistake 4: Treating all sessions the same
Short sessions should focus on one tier catch-up. Long sessions can fund full-round upgrades.

> **Tip:** Track your lowest two categories before every mission run. When you come back, spend with those two in mind first.
