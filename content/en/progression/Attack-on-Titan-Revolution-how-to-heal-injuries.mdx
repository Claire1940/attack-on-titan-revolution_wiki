## Understanding Injury Mechanics in 2026

Injuries in Attack on Titan Revolution are triggered by specific environmental hazards and Titan interactions. Unlike standard health depletion, an "Injury" often carries a debuff that persists until specifically addressed. For instance, leg injuries might prevent you from dashing, while arm injuries can slow down your blade swing speed.

In the current 2026 meta, players often intentionally trigger "Chronic Injuries" or "Injury Pro" modifiers to increase their gold and XP rewards. While this makes the game harder, it necessitates a deep understanding of the healing systems available.

## Attack on Titan Revolution: How to Heal Injuries Mid-Mission

The most critical time to heal is while you are still on the battlefield. You cannot always wait for the mission to end to fix a broken limb. There are two primary ways to handle this during active gameplay.

### 1. Utilizing Supply Stations (Spawn Points)
Every map features a dedicated "Replenish" icon, typically located where you first spawned or at designated outposts. By returning to this icon, you can fully restore your gas, blades, and health. This is the most reliable way to address **Attack on Titan Revolution how to heal injuries** without spending gems or rare resources.

### 2. Map-Wide Supply Crates
On larger maps like the Giant Forest, supply crates are scattered in fixed locations. Look for the "Box" icon on your HUD. Interacting with these will not only give you fresh blades but will also patch up minor injuries that might be slowing you down.

## Post-Mission Healing and Maintenance

Once a mission concludes, you are presented with a results screen. This is a pivotal moment for character maintenance. You will see options to "Retry," "Quit," or "Heal." 

If you have sustained heavy damage or are playing with the "Chronic Injuries" modifier active, you should select the Heal option before jumping into the next match. This ensures you start the next round with maximum HP and no lingering debuffs. Failing to do this can result in starting a new mission with a "Limp" status, which is almost a certain death sentence against high-level Titans.

### Gear Upgrades to Prevent Injuries
Prevention is often better than a cure. By upgrading your equipment, you can reduce the likelihood of sustaining an injury in the first place. High durability and better ODM speed allow you to dodge attacks that would otherwise break your character's bones.

| Gear Stat | Benefit for Survival | Upgrade Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ODM Speed** | Allows for faster dodging and eye-blinding maneuvers. | **High** |
| **Blade Durability** | Reduces the need to visit supply stations frequently. | **Medium** |
| **ODM Range** | Lets you stay further away from the Titan's grab range. | **Medium** |
| **Crit Chance** | Ends fights faster, reducing the window for Titan retaliation. | **Low** |
